## Situation

A high jump athlete looked tired during the session.

When more technical jump attempts were added, the athlete’s rhythm and movement quality became worse.

## What Theory Suggested

Training load and fatigue should be monitored because excessive fatigue can reduce performance and movement quality.

If fatigue is too high, the coach may need to reduce volume, adjust intensity, or change the session goal.

## What Happened In Practice

When the number of jumps was reduced and the focus shifted to approach rhythm, the athlete’s movement quality improved.

The athlete seemed to perform better when the session became more focused and less fatiguing.

## Lesson Learned

More technical attempts are not always better.

When fatigue is affecting movement quality, reducing jump volume and focusing on rhythm may produce a better session outcome.

## Possible Decision Rule

- If a high jump athlete is tired and rhythm becomes worse during technical jumping, then reduce jump volume and shift focus to approach rhythm because this may protect movement quality while still developing useful technical consistency.

## Follow-Up

Next time an athlete looks tired, observe:

- does rhythm drop first?
- does takeoff quality drop?
- does reducing volume improve movement?
- does the athlete respond better to rhythm work than full jumps?
- does the same pattern happen again?

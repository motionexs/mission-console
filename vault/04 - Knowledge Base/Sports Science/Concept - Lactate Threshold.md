## Simple Definition

Lactate threshold is the exercise intensity where lactate starts building up faster in the blood.

It answers the question:

How hard can the athlete work before fatigue starts increasing quickly?

## Technical Definition

Lactate threshold is the speed, workload, or percentage of VO2 max where blood lactate concentration begins to rise above resting levels.

It is important because it shows how much intensity an athlete can sustain before fatigue increases more rapidly.

## Why It Matters

Lactate threshold matters because athletes with a higher lactate threshold can maintain a faster pace or higher intensity for longer.

Two athletes may have similar VO2 max values, but the athlete with the better lactate threshold may perform better in endurance events.

This is because they can work at a higher percentage of their VO2 max without accumulating too much lactate.

## Sport Example

In long-distance running, an athlete with a higher lactate threshold can run faster for longer before fatigue forces them to slow down.

In high jump, lactate threshold is not a main performance quality, but understanding it helps me understand endurance training, conditioning, fatigue, and exercise intensity.

## Coaching Application

When coaching, lactate threshold helps me think about:

- endurance performance
- training intensity
- fatigue management
- conditioning sessions
- when an athlete is working too hard for too long
- how to prescribe aerobic or tempo work
- how to avoid turning recovery work into hard fatigue work

## Decision Rules

- If the goal is endurance performance, then improving lactate threshold may be important.
- If an athlete reaches fatigue quickly during sustained work, then lactate threshold may be a limiting factor.
- If training intensity is above lactate threshold, then duration should usually be shorter.
- If training intensity is below lactate threshold, then duration can usually be longer.
- If the goal is recovery, then avoid making the session too intense.
- If working with a power athlete, use lactate threshold knowledge carefully so endurance work supports training without reducing speed or power quality.

## Common Mistakes

- Thinking VO2 max is the only important endurance factor.
- Making every endurance session too hard.
- Ignoring the relationship between intensity and duration.
- Using high-intensity conditioning when the goal is recovery.
- Applying endurance methods to power athletes without considering their sport demands.
- Confusing general tiredness with specific lactate threshold limitations.
